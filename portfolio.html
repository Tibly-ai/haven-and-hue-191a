Our Work

Before, after, and everything in between

Every project below started with an empty room, a tired listing, or a "we're not sure where to begin." Here's how each one ended — and what happened when it hit the market.

Before After
Vacant Staging · Laurelhurst

The Maplewood Craftsman

4 Bed · 2 Bath One-Day Install Sold in 6 Days
Before

Freshly painted but echoing — buyers walked through in under ten minutes.

After

Warm walnut, linen, and terracotta drew buyers straight to the bay window.

This 1926 craftsman had beautiful bones — original fir floors, a river-stone fireplace, generous windows — but standing empty, it read as small and dark. Our job was to give the rooms a sense of scale and let that Portland light do the talking.

We anchored the living room with a low, wide sofa to open the sightline to the fireplace, layered in wool and linen textures, and styled the awkward alcove off the kitchen as a reading nook — the detail nearly every buyer mentioned in feedback. Three offers arrived within the first week.

  • Days on Market6 days
  • Final Price9% over asking
  • Rooms Staged7 rooms + porch
Occupied Staging · West Linn

Willow Creek Farmhouse

5 Bed · 3 Bath Family in Residence 12% Over Asking
Before

A well-loved family home — with a decade of everyday life filling every surface.

After

Edited, softened, and re-centered around the farmhouse's original heart: the kitchen.

The Hendersons were still living in the home while it sold — with two kids, a dog, and a very full calendar. Occupied stagings are a balancing act: the house has to photograph beautifully at 10am and still function as a family home at 6pm.

We edited roughly a third of the furnishings into storage, rearranged what remained to widen the traffic flow, and layered in artwork, bedding, and greenery from our inventory. The family kept living their life; the listing photos looked like a magazine spread. It went pending after the first open house.

  • Days on Market9 days
  • Final Price12% over asking
  • Items Edited OutOne full storage pod
Vacant Staging · Downtown Portland

The Ninth Street Loft

2 Bed · 2 Bath Open-Plan Concrete Sold in One Weekend
Before

Twelve-foot ceilings, poured concrete, and no clue where the "rooms" were.

After

Rugs and lighting carved the open plan into a living room, dining zone, and studio.

Lofts are our favorite puzzle. All that open space is a selling point on paper, but in person, buyers often can't imagine how to live in it. This one had sat vacant for seven weeks before the listing agent called us.

We used oversized rugs, a sculptural pendant, and a bookshelf placed perpendicular to the windows to define three distinct zones without a single wall. Warm caramel leather and soft clay tones took the chill off the concrete. The listing relaunched on a Friday; it was under contract by Sunday night.

  • Days on Market3 days (relaunch)
  • Final PriceFull asking, cash
  • Zones Created3 from one open plan
Interior Design · Sellwood

The Alder Street Refresh

Living + Dining Design to Stay Completed in 5 Weeks
Before

A "someday room" the owners had walked past for six years without settling into.

After

Sage, oak, and unlacquered brass — now the room where everyone ends up.

Not every transformation ends with a sold sign. Theo and Jamie bought their Sellwood bungalow in 2019 and never quite finished the main floor — a mismatched sofa here, a hand-me-down table there, and a lingering feeling that the room belonged to someone else.

We started with how they actually live: slow weekend mornings, dinners that migrate to the sofa, a growing record collection. The design followed — a deep sage accent wall, a custom oak console for the turntable, and layered lighting that makes the room glow after dusk. Same house, entirely new feeling.

  • Timeline5 weeks, concept to install
  • ScopeLiving & dining rooms
  • Kept & Reworked40% of existing pieces
