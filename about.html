About Haven & Hue

The people behind the exhale

We're a small Portland studio with a simple obsession: making homes feel calm, honest, and quietly beautiful — whether they're heading to market or staying in the family for good.

Our Story

It started with one empty house

In 2016, Mara Ellison walked through a vacant listing that had lingered on the market for four months. The bones were lovely — tall windows, oak floors, morning light — but the rooms felt hollow, and buyers couldn't imagine a life inside them. She borrowed a truckload of furniture, styled it over a long weekend, and the house sold nine days later.

"Nothing about the house changed. What changed was how it made people feel."

That weekend became Haven & Hue. Nearly a decade and more than 250 homes later, we've grown from one borrowed truck into a full staging warehouse and a three-person design team — but the heart of the work is the same. We help people feel something the moment they walk through a door.

Today we serve sellers, realtors, and homeowners across Portland and the surrounding valley, pairing real-estate practicality with a designer's patience for texture, light, and breathing room.

“Est. 2016 · Portland, Oregon · 250+ homes and counting.”
Our Philosophy

Design that feels like a deep breath

Trends come and go, but calm never goes out of style. Three principles guide every room we touch.

01

Warmth Over Wow

We don't chase dramatic showpieces. Layered textiles, natural materials, and soft neutrals invite people to stay a while — and that lingering feeling is what sells homes and settles hearts.

02

Room to Breathe

Every space needs negative space. We edit as much as we add, letting light travel and sightlines open so each room can speak in its own quiet voice.

03

Honest to the House

A 1920s craftsman shouldn't be dressed like a downtown loft. We listen to a home's architecture, era, and light, then design in a way that feels like it was always meant to be there.
